Here is a breakdown of the costs to set up this shop in South Africa.

**Important Note on Brands in SA:** While Lincoln and Miller are excellent, they are considered industrial/pro tier in South Africa and are extremely expensive (often R20,000+ just for the machine). For an amateur building gates, the "Pro-DIY" brands like **MAC AFRIC (Adendorff)**, **Matweld**, **Pinnacle**, and **Ryobi** are the standard choice.

Here are the estimated prices in **ZAR (Rands)** based on current pricing at major retailers like *Adendorff Machinery Mart*, *Builders Warehouse*, *Leroy Merlin*, and *Takealot*.

### 1. The MIG Welder
You need a machine that can run Gas MIG and takes a 5kg spool of wire.
*   **Recommendation:** Look for a 160A to 200A Inverter MIG.
*   **Top Pick:** MAC AFRIC 200A MIG/Stick Inverter (Available at Adendorff).
*   **Price:** **R 4,000 – R 5,500**

### 2. Gas Bottle (Argon/CO2 Mix)
*Note on South African Gas:* Traditionally, you had to rent cylinders from Afrox (paying a monthly rental fee). For a hobbyist, this is a waste of money. You should look for **"Purchase"** or **"Deposit"** cylinders (like *Albee* or *Flogas*). You buy the bottle once, and then just swap it for a full one.
*   **Item:** Integrated Valve Cylinder (mix for Mig).
*   **Price (First time buy):** **R 3,500 – R 4,500**
*   **Refills:** ~R 600 – R 800

### 3. Auto-Darkening Helmet
Do not skimp here, but you don't need a professional Speedglas.
*   **Brands:** Pinnacle, Matweld, Promax.
*   **Price:** **R 700 (Entry) – R 1,500 (Mid-range)**

### 4. Angle Grinder & Discs
Get a 115mm (4.5") grinder.
*   **Grinder (Ryobi / Bosch Green / Makita MT):** R 600 – R 1,000.
*   **Flap Discs (Zirconia is best):** R 30 – R 50 each (Get 5).
*   **Cutting Discs:** R 150 for a pack of 10.
*   **Total for Kit:** **~R 1,200**

### 5. Chop Saw (Cut-off Saw)
You need a 355mm Abrasive Cut-off saw.
*   **Brands:** Ryobi, MAC AFRIC, Ingco.
*   **Price:** **R 2,400 – R 3,500**

### 6. Welding Magnets
*   **Price:** R 80 – R 150 each.
*   **Total (for 4):** **~R 500**

### 7. Respirator
In SA, we use the European standard (FFP2/FFP3) rather than the US "P100" terminology, though 3M uses both.
*   **Option A (Disposable):** Box of FFP2 Welding masks with valves (3M or Honeywell). **R 400**
*   **Option B (Reusable - Recommended):** 3M 6000 Series Half Mask (~R 350) + 3M 2135 Filters (P3 rating) (~R 250).
*   **Total:** **~R 600**

### Where to shop in SA:
1.  **Adendorff Machinery Mart:** This is your best friend. They sell the *MAC AFRIC* range which is arguably the best "bang for buck" for semi-pro welding in SA. Go here for the welder, magnets, and chop saw.
2.  **Builders Warehouse:** Good for Ryobi grinders, paint, and sometimes steel.
3.  **Local Steel Merchants:** **Do not buy steel from Builders Warehouse**; it is too expensive. Look for local steel merchants (like *Najaar*, *Macsteel*, or smaller independent steel yards) in your industrial area. They sell lengths of tubing for much cheaper.
4.  **Specialized Welding Shops:** Look for shops like *Welding Cutting Services* or *Coastal Welding* for your gas and wire if you want expert advice.
